How it works

Scan AML checks exposure in three steps

The flow is built for practical AML standards in crypto: paste public data, run the screening, then review the risk context before you decide what to do next.

1

Paste public on-chain data

Enter a wallet address or transaction hash from Ethereum or another supported network. Scan AML only needs public chain data, so your private keys stay out of the workflow.

2

Run the read-only check

The app compares the address path with sanctions references, watchlist signals, and on-chain transaction history without asking you to sign.

3

Review the result

Open the result in the app to see the screening context, relevant exposure categories, and why a wallet or transaction deserves a closer look.

What it checks

Risk signals built for DeFi decisions

Scan AML helps users screen token transfers, wallet counterparties, treasury addresses, and transaction hashes before interacting with contracts or moving funds.

Sanctions & watchlists

Check whether a wallet or transaction has exposure to OFAC sanctions, blocked entities, or watchlist references that matter for compliance review.

On-chain risk exposure

Spot links to mixers, scams, exploited contracts, darknet markets, and other illicit-source categories that can affect wallet hygiene and counterparty confidence.

Assets & chains

Use the checker for Ethereum wallets, ETH transfers, ERC-20 assets, stablecoin activity, and other ERC-20 token flows supported in the app.

FAQ

Scan AML questions

01

What is Scan AML?

Scan AML is a non-custodial DeFi checker for screening wallet addresses and transactions for sanctions exposure, illicit-source links, and other AML risk indicators.

02

How long does a screening take?

The preview is designed for quick checks, while the live result appears in the app. Timing depends on the address, chain activity, and available network data.

03

Do I need to connect a wallet?

No. Scan AML is read-only for screening: paste an address or transaction hash, run the check, and review the result without connecting a wallet or signing a message.

04

What does it cost?

Open the app to see any current access limits, plan details, or usage terms before you run a live check. This page does not quote pricing or fees.

05

Which chains and assets are supported?

Scan AML focuses on Ethereum-style wallet and transaction screening, with supported assets and networks shown in the app. See the supported chains page for the local overview.

06

Is an AML result a legal verdict?

No. A screening result is a due-diligence signal, not a final legal conclusion. For regulated activity, combine app results with your own policies and financial crime compliance guidance.
